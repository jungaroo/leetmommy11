Equity

A warning

  • Unless youâre at a publicly traded or large company, your equity often has no/little value at the time you receive it.
  • Equity is given over a multi-year period, usually four years.
    This is called a vesting schedule.
  • If you leave a company within the first year, you almost always forfeit your equity. This is called a cliff.

Equity

Equity is commonly granted in one of two ways:

  • Options
    • The option to purchase a stock at a specific price (called the strike price)
    • The owner of an option can choose to âexerciseâ (purchase the stock) or not

  • RSU
    • More common with larger companies, instead of the option you are given stock.
    • When you are given stock, it is treated like income so you must pay taxes on it

Important Notes

  • Company Valuation
    • Always ask what the latest valuation of the company was
  • Ownership
    • Always ask what percentage in the company your equity/options correspond to
  • Strike Price

  • Taxes
    • Be mindful, this is an issue with RSUs
